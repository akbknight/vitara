The experience

From first input
to a plan that
fits your life.

Onboarding takes under four minutes. The plan you see at the end should feel like it was written for you — because it was.

Step one

Tell VITARA
who you are.

Onboarding is the most critical experience in VITARA. It collects the inputs that make the first plan feel personal. The flow is designed to be conversational, fast, and to telegraph value at every step — each screen should make you feel increasingly seen, not interrogated.

Each step is designed to be immediately intuitive. No medical terminology. No intimidating forms. Just questions that feel like they're from someone who actually understands your situation.

Step two

Get your
adaptive plan.

A meal and exercise plan that reflects your actual food, your actual environment, and your actual body clock. Not a template. Not an average. Yours.

This is the moment we are engineering for: the end of your first onboarding session, when you see the plan and feel — not just register, but viscerally feel — that this was made for you.

Example — South Asian user, DC, night owl

Monday meals

9:00 AM — Poha with peanuts, green chili, curry leaves (340 kcal)
1:30 PM — Dal makhani + brown rice + cucumber raita (520 kcal)
8:00 PM — Aloo methi sabzi + 2 rotis + small bowl of dahi (460 kcal)

Monday exercise

11:00 AM — 25-min yoga flow (peak energy window)
6:30 PM — 30-min walk (DC temperature at this hour: 64°F)

Why this plan

Your chronotype peaks at 11 AM and again at 5–7 PM. DC in October averages 62°F — comfortable for outdoor walks but cold for early morning runs. Meals are timed to your actual wake cycle.

Step three

Coaching that
actually adjusts.

As you use VITARA, the plan evolves. Your feedback, your progress, and your changing context keep the plan relevant. This is not a set-it-and-forget-it plan generator. It is a coaching relationship.

Conversational AI Coach

Ask questions, get explanations, report what worked and what didn't. The AI coach reads your context — it knows your food culture, your goals, and your progress — before it answers.

Weekly Plan Updates

Every week, the plan is reviewed and adjusted based on your feedback, logged data, and progress. Stagnant plans are the primary reason people quit health apps.

Context-Aware Scheduling

Ramadan starts. You're traveling. Your sleep shifted. VITARA adapts — not because you manually reprogrammed everything, but because the system tracks your context and adjusts automatically.

The bar

The moment of
recognition.

"This is the first health app that has ever understood me."

That is the bar. Not "this looks reasonable." If users finish onboarding and think the plan looks reasonable, we failed. The bar is recognition — the visceral feeling that this was built for exactly who you are.

Under the hood

Powered by large
language models.

VITARA uses Claude (Anthropic) as its conversational AI backbone, with a culturally-enriched knowledge base built specifically for the communities we serve.

The cultural intelligence is not just prompt engineering. It is a retrieval-augmented knowledge base with South Asian, West African, Middle Eastern, and Latin American food and health research — built into the system before any user message is processed.

How a plan is generated

1

Context assembly

Onboarding data (ethnicity, location, sleep, goals, restrictions) assembled into a structured user context object.

2

Cultural knowledge retrieval

RAG system retrieves relevant cultural food and health context from the VITARA knowledge base. This is where "South Asian food culture + diabetes risk" becomes specific, grounded information.

3

Plan generation via Claude API

Structured prompt with user context + retrieved knowledge → Claude generates a weekly meal and exercise plan with reasoning.

4

Plan review + presentation

The generated plan is parsed, stored, and presented in the app's Today screen. The user sees meals by name, timing, and rationale — not JSON.
